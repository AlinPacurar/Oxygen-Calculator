All values should be independently verified before transfer. Ensure cylinder pressures, device performance, reserve requirements, patient condition, route delays, and escalation contingencies are considered before use.

Why These Formulas Were Chosen ▾

Spontaneously breathing patients: For simple oxygen devices in UK practice, a common teaching estimate is oxygen use as flow × time, then multiplied by around 1.5–2 as a transport or planning margin. The formula (flow × time) × 2 follows that conservative approach.

Ventilated and CPAP patients: Guidance commonly allows for extra fixed flow beyond minute volume alone, because ventilators and CPAP systems use more gas than patient minute volume by itself. The ventilated formula ((minute volume + 3) × FiO₂ × time) × 2 is a simplified conservative planning rule that adds extra flow before applying reserve.

CPAP: The added leak + 7 term reflects that CPAP circuits generally have higher baseline flow and greater leak than standard invasive ventilation. This is intended as a cautious approximation rather than an exact device-specific consumption value.

High-flow systems: The formula (flow × FiO₂ × time) × 2 follows the principle that oxygen consumption is approximately related to total delivered flow multiplied by FiO₂, with the final ×2 acting as a safety buffer.
